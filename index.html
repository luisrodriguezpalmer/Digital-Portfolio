Research Abstract

The Math Behind Google’s PageRank Algorithm

Luis Rodriguez, Roxana Martell, Maria Romero, and Luis Rodriguez, M.S.,

Miami Dade College, Padron Campus, Miami, FL

Abstract

The Google Matrix is a fundamental tool in computing the PageRank vector, which ranks webpages by their importance. It’s built from the structure of hyperlinks across the web and models the behavior of a “random surfer” who either follows a link on a page or jumps to a random site. The dominant eigenvector of this matrix gives the steady-state distribution of the surfer’s location, the PageRank vector. In our project, we focused on constructing the Google Matrix from an initial hyperlink graph, incorporating a damping factor to simulate random jumps, and resolving challenges like sink nodes and dead ends. Once the matrix was built, we computed its steady-state vector using both iterative and analytical approaches. Finally, we analyzed how different link structures affected convergence speed and ranking stability.
